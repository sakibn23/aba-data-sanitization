## SESSION SUMMARY

### Activities and Interventions

Jacob participated in afternoon programming at his residential setting. Staff implemented individualized universal protocol with access to preferred items including outdoor time, basketball, and staff attention. The session began with planned outdoor recreation time. However, at approximately 3:25 PM, a peer antagonized Jacob by taking his basketball without permission, which escalated to physical aggression requiring the use of SCIP-R restrictive physical intervention.

Prior to the incident, Jacob was engaging appropriately with outdoor activities and demonstrated positive mood. After being antagonized by his peer, Jacob quickly escalated from verbal aggression to physical aggression directed at the peer. Staff implemented all interventions outlined in Jacob's BSP including SCIP-R verbal and non-verbal calming techniques, removal of the peer from the area, and statements of support. However, Jacob's behavior continued to escalate to a level that presented an immediate health and safety risk.

A two-person SCIP-R supine restraint was implemented at 3:28 PM lasting approximately 4 minutes. Following the intervention, staff completed all required post-intervention procedures including body check by nursing staff, contact with clinician, and contact with program administrator. Jacob was able to calm after the intervention and engaged appropriately for the remainder of the session.
